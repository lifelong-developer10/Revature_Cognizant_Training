Latest Housing Market News: Price Trends & StabilizationHousing prices in many urban areas are showing signs of stabilizing after a period of rapid growth. Experts note that home price increases have slowed down recently, suggesting a shift toward a more balanced market where neither buyers nor sellers dominate. In some cities, prices are expected to ease or even dip slightly in the coming year, driven by higher inventory levels, modest mortgage rates, and more measured demand.
Metro Rail Expansion Approved The government has officially approved a major expansion of the metro rail network aimed at improving urban mobility and reducing road congestion. The new phase of development will connect previously underserved residential and industrial areas, making daily travel faster, safer, and more affordable for commuters.
Chennai Plans 100 Smart Signals by Dec 31 Several cities are now introducing smart traffic signals to improve traffic management and reduce congestion. These signals use AI, sensors, and cameras to detect real-time traffic flow and dynamically adjust signal timings instead of relying on fixed cycles. For example, Ludhiana and Chennai in India are installing smart signals at busy intersections.

Kolkata Housing Market Sees Sales Drop 12% as New Launches Rise Housing sales in Kolkata declined by about 12 % in 2025 compared with last year, reflecting a slowdown in demand across India’s residential property markets amid economic uncertainties and job market pressures. Despite this drop in sales, new housing launches surged by 31 %, with most units priced below ₹1.5 crore to attract middle‑income buyers and keep affordability in focus. Unsold inventories also climbed, indicating more homes are on the market even as demand softens. Industry experts expect the market to recover in 2026 with improved regulations, lower interest rates, and renewed buyer confidence.

Sports Update: India Announce Under‑19 Cricket World Cup Squad

India has named its Under‑19 cricket squad for the upcoming ICC Men’s U19 World Cup 2026, with Ayush Mhatre appointed as captain and Vaibhav Sooryavanshi leading the team on the South Africa tour. This announcement reflects a focus on nurturing young talent as the team prepares for major international competition, giving promising players valuable leadership and match experience ahead of the World Cup. The Indian Women’s Hockey Team secured their spot in the 2026 Asian Games after a thrilling victory against Japan in the qualifying rounds. The team displayed exceptional skill and teamwork, with forward Rani Rampal leading the attack. This qualification marks another step in India’s push to strengthen women’s hockey on the international stage and inspires young athletes across the country
